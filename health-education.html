Health Education: Maternal and Child Health

üìå What is Maternal Health?

Maternal health refers to the health of women during pregnancy, childbirth, and the postpartum period. It involves maintaining proper nutrition, emotional support, regular health checkups, and access to safe delivery services.

‚úÖ Good Practices for Mothers:

  • Regular antenatal visits to monitor fetal growth and maternal health.
  • Eating a balanced diet rich in iron, folic acid, and calcium.
  • Avoiding harmful substances (alcohol, tobacco, certain medications).
  • Getting screened for infections such as HIV, syphilis, and malaria.

üßí Child Health: A Foundation for Life

Child health includes the physical, emotional, and cognitive well-being of children from birth through adolescence. Early investment in healthcare, nutrition, education, and emotional development shapes future success.

ü©∫ Key Areas of Focus:

  • Immunization: Protects against life-threatening diseases like measles, polio, and diphtheria.
  • Nutrition: Breastfeeding, vitamin A, and iron-rich foods support development.
  • Hygiene: Handwashing, clean water, and sanitation prevent infections.
  • Development: Engage children with talking, singing, and play to boost cognitive growth.

üß≠ Key Tips for Parents

  • Begin antenatal care as soon as pregnancy is confirmed.
  • Keep a growth and vaccination chart for your child.
  • Breastfeed exclusively for the first 6 months and continue up to 2 years with solid food.
  • Seek medical care immediately if your child shows signs of fever, dehydration, or developmental delay.
  • Practice mental health self-care for yourself and bond with your baby.
